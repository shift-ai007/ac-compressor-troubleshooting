# AC Compressor Repair vs. Replace: Cost Analysis Guide

The compressor is the most expensive single component in a residential AC system. When it fails, the repair-or-replace decision can mean a difference of $2,000 to $6,000. This guide breaks down the real costs so you can make an informed choice.

## Quick Decision Framework

Before diving into numbers, answer these three questions:

1. **How old is your AC system?** If it's over 12 years, replacing the entire system almost always makes more financial sense than replacing just the compressor.
2. **What refrigerant does it use?** R-22 systems are on borrowed time — R-22 is no longer manufactured, and remaining stock costs $100-200 per pound. Converting or replacing is the only viable long-term option.
3. **Is it still under warranty?** Compressor warranties typically run 5-10 years. Parts-only warranties still leave you paying $800-1,500 in labor.

## Repair Cost Breakdown

### Common Compressor Repairs (Not Full Replacement)

| Repair | Parts | Labor | Total Range |
|--------|-------|-------|-------------|
| Capacitor replacement | $15-45 | $100-200 | $115-245 |
| Contactor replacement | $25-75 | $100-200 | $125-275 |
| Hard start kit installation | $30-100 | $75-150 | $105-250 |
| Compressor terminal repair | $50-150 | $200-400 | $250-550 |
| Refrigerant recharge (R-410A) | $50-150 | $100-200 | $150-350 |
| Leak detection and repair | $100-300 | $200-500 | $300-800 |

These repairs fix components around the compressor, not the compressor itself. Total cost rarely exceeds $800.

### Compressor Replacement (Unit Stays)

Replacing just the compressor while keeping the rest of the outdoor and indoor units:

| Component | Cost Range |
|-----------|-----------|
| Compressor (part) | $800-2,000 |
| Refrigerant (R-410A, 8-12 lbs) | $200-500 |
| Labor (4-8 hours) | $600-1,500 |
| Filter drier replacement | $50-150 |
| Miscellaneous (brazing, nitrogen, vacuum) | $100-300 |
| **Total** | **$1,750-4,450** |

**Important**: A new compressor in an old system is like a new engine in a car with 200,000 miles. The compressor is new, but the condenser coils, fan motors, TXV, evaporator coil, and ductwork are all original. Other components may fail within 1-2 years.

## The 5,000-Dollar Question

Here's the math most homeowners need:

**Scenario: 10-year-old 3-ton R-410A system, compressor failure, no warranty.**

- **Option A — Compressor only**: $2,800 average. System continues running but is 10 years old. Remaining components have 3-5 years of expected life.
- **Option B — Full replacement**: $7,500 average. Everything is new. 10-year parts warranty, 14-16 SEER2 minimum (compared to your old 13 SEER unit).

Option B costs $4,700 more upfront. But:

- Energy savings of $30-60/month with higher SEER2 rating = $360-720/year
- No risk of other components failing and requiring additional service calls
- New warranty covers everything for 5-10 years
- $4,700 spread over 15 years of system life = $26/month

Over 10 years, Option B typically costs less in total when you factor in energy savings, avoided repairs, and warranty coverage.

## When Repair Makes Sense

- System is less than 7 years old
- Uses R-410A (or newer) refrigerant
- Compressor is still under warranty (you pay labor only)
- Rest of the system is in good condition (clean coils, good airflow, no duct leaks)
- The repair is a supporting component (capacitor, contactor, hard start kit), not the compressor itself

## When Replacement Makes Sense

- System is 10+ years old
- Uses R-22 refrigerant
- Compressor warranty has expired
- This is the second major repair in 2 years
- Energy bills have been climbing despite maintenance
- System uses more than 14 SEER (new minimum is higher)
- You plan to stay in the home for 5+ years

## The R-22 Factor

If your system uses R-22 (Freon), the math is simple:

- R-22 is no longer manufactured (EPA phaseout completed January 2020)
- Remaining reclaimed stock costs $100-200 per pound
- A typical recharge needs 8-12 pounds = $800-2,400 just for refrigerant
- R-22 systems are 15+ years old by definition
- Every R-22 repair is throwing money at a system you'll have to replace anyway

**Our recommendation**: Any R-22 compressor failure should trigger a full system replacement. The economics don't support keeping R-22 alive.

## Financing Options

Most HVAC companies offer financing for system replacements:

- **0% APR for 12-18 months** — common promotional rate, great if you can pay it off in time
- **Low-rate loans (4-8% APR)** — 5-10 year terms, monthly payments of $80-150 for a typical system
- **Utility rebates** — FPL and other Florida utilities offer $150-400 rebates for high-efficiency systems
- **Federal tax credits** — Energy-efficient HVAC systems may qualify for federal tax credits (check IRS guidelines for current year)

## Getting Accurate Quotes

When comparing quotes for compressor or system replacement:

1. Get at least 3 written quotes
2. Ensure quotes specify the exact equipment model numbers
3. Verify the installer is licensed (Florida requires a CAC license for HVAC work)
4. Ask about warranty terms — both manufacturer and labor warranty
5. Check if the quote includes permit fees (required by Florida law for system replacements)
6. Ask about disposal of the old equipment
